Darwin Wu

Look at someone's face.

It should be dynamic and in motion.

You should be able to see both eyes,

but they should not only be looking directly at you.

Now focus your eyes on the face.

Don’t focus too hard or too little.

Find the sweet spot where the face is the only thing your eyes perceive

and everything else in your sight is unfocused and uninteresting.

Then focus on specific parts of the face.

See the eyes wander,

ears twitch, mouth move, forehead wrinkle, cheek distort.

Ponder the perfect placement yet imperfect proportion,

the beautiful whole yet flawed detail.

Look at that face

—anyone’s face—

and tell me you don’t love it.
